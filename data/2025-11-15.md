### [1] [Know Your Limits: Entropy Estimation Modeling for Compression and Generalization](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.10618)
*Benjamin L. Badger,Matthew Neligeorge*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: 该研究提出了编码器增强型因果解码器模型架构，以克服语言预测的信息熵限制，并在计算效率和语言压缩方面取得了优于传统因果Transformer的性能。研究还展示了如何获得逐个token的熵估计，并证明了训练模型接近熵而不是最小化损失可以提高泛化能力。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 语言预测受到其内在信息熵的限制，这既限制了语言模型的准确性上限，也限制了语言压缩的下限。现有的最有效的压缩算法是因果（下一个token预测）大语言模型，但计算它们准确的语言熵估计在计算上是不可行的。因此，需要新的模型架构和方法来更有效地估计语言熵并突破现有压缩瓶颈。

Method: 研究引入了编码器增强型因果解码器模型架构，旨在提高训练效率和压缩性能。该架构能够获得逐个token的熵估计。实验将这种新模型与标准的因果Transformer模型进行比较，并在适度的硬件上进行训练，以评估其在语言压缩和泛化能力方面的表现。研究还对比了两种训练策略：一种是训练模型接近其训练数据的估计熵，另一种是最小化损失。

Result: 与标准的因果Transformer模型相比，编码器增强型因果解码器模型在适度的硬件上实现了更高的压缩率。研究证明，将模型训练目标设定为接近估计的逐个token熵，而不是简单地最小化损失，可以显著提高模型的泛化能力。实验结果表明，这种基于熵的训练方法优于未考虑熵的传统训练方法。

Conclusion: 该研究成功提出了一种更有效的语言模型架构和训练方法，能够更准确地估计语言熵，并实现更好的语言压缩和泛化能力。编码器增强型因果解码器模型在计算效率和性能上均优于现有方法，为未来的语言建模和压缩技术指明了方向。未来的工作可以进一步探索该架构在不同规模的数据集和任务上的应用，并研究更复杂的熵估计技术。

Abstract: Language prediction is constrained by informational entropy intrinsic to language, such that there exists a limit to how accurate any language model can become and equivalently a lower bound to language compression. The most efficient language compression algorithms today are causal (next token prediction) large language models, but the use of these models to form accurate estimates of language entropy is currently computationally infeasible. We introduce encoder-augmented causal decoder model architectures that exhibit superior training efficiency characteristics and achieve higher compression than causal transformers even when trained on modest hardware. We demonstrate how entropy estimates can be obtained on a per-token basis, and show that the generalization of models trained to approach the entropy of their training data necessarily exceeds the generalization of models trained to minimize loss beyond this value. We show empirically that causal models trained to approach but not exceed estimated per-token entropies exhibit greater generalization than models trained without taking entropy into account.

### [2] [SSR: Socratic Self-Refine for Large Language Model Reasoning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.10621)
*Haizhou Shi,Ye Liu,Bo Pang,Zeyu Leo Liu,Hao Wang,Silvio Savarese,Caiming Xiong,Yingbo Zhou,Semih Yavuz*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: 本研究提出了Socratic Self-Refine (SSR)框架，用于改进大型语言模型（LLMs）在复杂推理任务中的评估和精炼。SSR通过将模型响应分解为可验证的“子问题-子答案”对，并进行精细的步级置信度评估和迭代优化，显著提高了推理的准确性和可解释性，优于现有方法。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 现有的大型语言模型（LLMs）在推理方面表现出色，但目前的测试时框架依赖于粗粒度的自我验证和纠正，这在处理复杂任务时效果有限。因此，需要一种更精细的评估和优化LLMs推理能力的方法。

Method: SSR框架将LLM的响应分解为一系列（子问题，子答案）对。通过受控的再求解和自洽性检查，对每个子问题的答案进行步级置信度评估。一旦发现不可靠的步骤，SSR会对其进行迭代式精炼，从而生成更准确、更具可解释性的推理链。该方法是一种黑盒方法，不依赖于模型内部的细节。

Result: 在五个推理基准和三个LLMs上的实验表明，SSR的性能始终优于最先进的迭代式自我精炼基线。SSR不仅提升了性能，还提供了一种原则性的方法来评估和理解LLMs的内部推理过程。

Conclusion: SSR框架通过精细化的评估和精确的精炼，有效提升了LLMs在复杂推理任务中的表现，并增强了推理过程的可解释性。未来可以进一步探索SSR在更多复杂任务和不同模型上的应用，以及优化其迭代精炼的策略。

Abstract: Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable reasoning abilities, yet existing test-time frameworks often rely on coarse self-verification and self-correction, limiting their effectiveness on complex tasks. In this paper, we propose Socratic Self-Refine (SSR), a novel framework for fine-grained evaluation and precise refinement of LLM reasoning. Our proposed SSR decomposes model responses into verifiable (sub-question, sub-answer) pairs, enabling step-level confidence estimation through controlled re-solving and self-consistency checks. By pinpointing unreliable steps and iteratively refining them, SSR produces more accurate and interpretable reasoning chains. Empirical results across five reasoning benchmarks and three LLMs show that SSR consistently outperforms state-of-the-art iterative self-refinement baselines. Beyond performance gains, SSR provides a principled black-box approach for evaluating and understanding the internal reasoning processes of LLMs. Code is available at https://github.com/SalesforceAIResearch/socratic-self-refine-reasoning.

Conclusion: Instella模型家族为社区提供了一个透明、高性能且多功能的开源替代方案，促进了开放和可复现的语言模型研究。它克服了现有模型闭源的限制，并在性能上取得了显著成就，尤其是在长文本处理和数学推理方面。

### [4] [ParoQuant: Pairwise Rotation Quantization for Efficient Reasoning LLM Inference](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.10645)
*Yesheng Liang,Haisheng Chen,Song Han,Zhijian Liu*

Main category: cs.CL

TL;DR: Weight-only post-training quantization (PTQ) methods compress LLMs but struggle with outliers causing accuracy degradation. ParoQuant, a novel weight-only PTQ method, uses Givens rotations and channel-wise scaling to mitigate outliers and reduce dynamic range. It achieves 2.4% accuracy improvement over AWQ on reasoning tasks with low overhead, enabling efficient LLM deployment.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Large Language Models (LLMs) require significant memory and computational resources for inference. Weight-only post-training quantization (PTQ) is a technique to compress LLMs by reducing the precision of their weights, thereby decreasing memory footprint and accelerating inference. However, a major challenge in PTQ is the presence of outlier values in the weights and activations of LLMs. These outliers can cause substantial quantization errors, leading to a significant drop in model accuracy. This problem is particularly acute in recent reasoning LLMs, where errors can propagate and accumulate across long chains of thought, further degrading performance. Existing PTQ methods often fall short in effectively suppressing these outliers or introduce considerable computational overhead during inference, negating the benefits of quantization.

Method: ParoQuant is a new weight-only PTQ method that addresses the limitations of existing approaches. It combines two key techniques: 1) Independent Givens rotations: These are hardware-efficient and optimizable rotations that help to even out the magnitude distribution across different channels. This process effectively suppresses outliers and narrows the dynamic range of the weights within each quantization group. 2) Channel-wise scaling: This technique further complements the rotations by applying scaling factors to each channel, ensuring a more uniform distribution of weight magnitudes. To maximize the efficiency of these operations, ParoQuant also involves a co-designed inference kernel that leverages GPU parallelism. This ensures that the rotations and scaling operations add minimal runtime overhead. The method is evaluated on reasoning tasks, and its performance is compared against existing methods like AWQ.

Result: ParoQuant demonstrates significant improvements in accuracy and efficiency compared to existing methods. Specifically, on reasoning tasks, ParoQuant achieves an average accuracy improvement of 2.4% over AWQ. Crucially, this improvement comes with minimal overhead, less than 10%, making it a practical solution for deploying LLMs. The results indicate that ParoQuant effectively handles outliers and reduces quantization errors without sacrificing performance.

Conclusion: ParoQuant presents a significant advancement in weight-only post-training quantization for Large Language Models. By innovatively combining pairwise Givens rotations and channel-wise scaling, it effectively addresses the challenge of outlier suppression and dynamic range reduction, leading to improved accuracy and efficiency. The method achieves superior performance on reasoning tasks compared to existing techniques like AWQ, with minimal runtime overhead. This work paves the way for more accessible and performant deployment of powerful reasoning LLMs, with potential implications for various natural language processing applications. Future work could explore the application of ParoQuant to other types of models or quantization strategies.

Abstract: Weight-only post-training quantization (PTQ) compresses the weights of Large Language Models (LLMs) into low-precision representations to reduce memory footprint and accelerate inference. However, the presence of outliers in weights and activations often leads to large quantization errors and severe accuracy degradation, especially in recent reasoning LLMs where errors accumulate across long chains of thought. Existing PTQ methods either fail to sufficiently suppress outliers or introduce significant overhead during inference. In this paper, we propose Pairwise Rotation Quantization (ParoQuant), a weight-only PTQ method that combines hardware-efficient and optimizable independent Givens rotations with channel-wise scaling to even out the magnitude across channels and narrow the dynamic range within each quantization group. We further co-design the inference kernel to fully exploit GPU parallelism and keep the rotations and scaling lightweight at runtime. ParoQuant achieves an average 2.4% accuracy improvement over AWQ on reasoning tasks with less than 10% overhead. This paves the way for more efficient and accurate deployment of reasoning LLMs.

### [5] [Querying Labeled Time Series Data with Scenario Programs](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.10627)

TL;DR: 模拟环境中的自动驾驶汽车（AV）故障场景可能无法在现实世界中重现，这主要是由于模拟与真实传感器数据之间的“模拟到现实”差距。本文提出了一种通过在真实世界数据集上定位和验证这些场景来解决此问题的方法。研究人员形式化了标签时间序列传感器数据与使用 Scenic 语言定义的抽象场景之间的匹配，并开发了一种查询算法来识别匹配数据的子集。实验结果表明，该算法在查询场景方面比最先进的商业视觉大型语言模型更准确、速度快几个数量级，并且可以随着查询时间序列数据的持续时间而扩展。


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: 自动驾驶汽车（AV）的安全至关重要，而基于仿真的测试是道路测试的重要补充。然而，在仿真环境中发现的故障场景是否能在真实系统中重现仍然是一个关键问题。仿真到现实的差距（sim-to-real gap）可能导致仿真中发现的故障场景要么是合成传感器数据的伪影，要么是实际存在的问题。因此，验证仿真故障场景的有效方法是检查这些场景在真实世界数据集中的出现情况，并确认故障是否依然存在。

The sim-to-real gap caused by differences between simulated and real sensor data means that failure scenarios identified in simulation might either be artifacts of synthetic sensor data or actual issues that also occur with real sensor data. To address this, an effective approach to validating simulated failure scenarios is to locate occurrences of these scenarios within real-world datasets and verify whether the failure persists on the datasets. To this end, we introduce a formal definition of how labeled time series sensor data can match an abstract scenario, represented as a scenario program using the Scenic probabilistic programming language. We present a querying algorithm that, given a scenario program and a labeled dataset, identifies the subset of data that matches the specified scenario. Our experiment shows that our algorithm is more accurate and orders of magnitude faster in querying scenarios than the state-of-the-art commercial vision large language models, and can scale with the duration of queried time series data.
