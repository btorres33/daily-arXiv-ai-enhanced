### [1] [BOTS: A Unified Framework for Bayesian Online Task Selection in LLM Reinforcement Finetuning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2510.26374)
*Qianli Shen,Daoyuan Chen,Yilun Huang,Zhenqing Ling,Yaliang Li,Bolin Ding,Jingren Zhou*

Main category: cs.AI

TL;DR: Reinforcement finetuning (RFT) for LLMs is sensitive to task selection. We introduce BOTS, a Bayesian online task selection framework that adaptively estimates task difficulty using explicit and implicit evidence. BOTS improves data efficiency and performance in RFT across diverse domains and LLM scales, outperforming baselines.

Motivation: Reinforcement finetuning (RFT) is crucial for aligning LLMs with human preferences and improving reasoning, but its success hinges on effective task selection during training. Uniform task sampling is inefficient, wasting resources on tasks that are too easy or too difficult. Existing task selection methods often struggle with high costs, poor adaptability, or insufficient evidence. This research addresses the critical need for an efficient and adaptive task selection strategy in RFT.

Method: We introduce BOTS (Bayesian Online Task Selection), a unified framework for LLM reinforcement finetuning. BOTS leverages Bayesian inference to maintain adaptive posterior estimates of task difficulty as the LLM evolves. It integrates explicit evidence from direct task evaluations and implicit evidence from these evaluations for unselected tasks. Thompson sampling is used to balance exploration and exploitation. An ultra-light interpolation-based plug-in is employed to estimate the difficulty of unevaluated tasks without additional rollouts, minimizing overhead.

Result: Empirical evaluations across various domains and LLM scales demonstrate that BOTS consistently enhances data efficiency and overall performance compared to existing baselines and ablation studies. The results highlight BOTS as a practical and scalable solution for dynamic task selection in RFT.

Conclusion: BOTS provides a principled and effective framework for Bayesian online task selection in LLM reinforcement finetuning. By adaptively estimating task difficulty and balancing exploration with exploitation, it significantly improves data efficiency and model performance. BOTS represents a practical and extensible advancement for optimizing the RFT process.

Abstract: Reinforcement finetuning (RFT) is a key technique for aligning Large Language
Models (LLMs) with human preferences and enhancing reasoning, yet its
effectiveness is highly sensitive to which tasks are explored during training.
Uniform task sampling is inefficient, wasting computation on tasks that are
either trivial or unsolvable, while existing task selection methods often
suffer from high rollout costs, poor adaptivity, or incomplete evidence. We
introduce BOTS, a unified framework for Bayesian Online Task Selection in LLM
reinforcement finetuning. Grounded in Bayesian inference, BOTS adaptively
maintains posterior estimates of task difficulty as the model evolves. It
jointly incorporates explicit evidence from direct evaluations of selected
tasks and implicit evidence inferred from these evaluations for unselected
tasks, with Thompson sampling ensuring a principled balance between exploration
and exploitation. To make implicit evidence practical, we instantiate it with
an ultra-light interpolation-based plug-in that estimates difficulties of
unevaluated tasks without extra rollouts, adding negligible overhead.
Empirically, across diverse domains and LLM scales, BOTS consistently improves
data efficiency and performance over baselines and ablations, providing a
practical and extensible solution for dynamic task selection in RFT.
